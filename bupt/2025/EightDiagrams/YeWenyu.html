About the Eight Diagrams (八卦)

The Eight Diagrams, known as Ba Gua (八卦) in Chinese, are a fundamental concept in Chinese philosophy and cosmology. Each trigram consists of three lines that can be either solid (Yang ☰) or broken (Yin ☰), representing different natural forces and phenomena.

At the center lies the Tai Chi (太極) symbol, representing the balance and interaction between Yin and Yang forces. The eight trigrams surrounding it form the basis of the 64 hexagrams found in the I Ching (易經), one of the oldest Chinese classical texts.

Hover over each trigram to see it highlighted. The traditional arrangement places Heaven (乾) at the top and Earth (坤) at the bottom, with the other six elements positioned around the octagon according to ancient cosmological principles.
